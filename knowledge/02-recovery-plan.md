## The structural fix: Arobs Parent Company Guarantee

The single most important structural change is establishing a standing Arobs Group Parent Company Guarantee arrangement, ready to drop into any UK public sector submission.

What this means in practice:

- Engage Arobs Group legal and finance. The PCG is a legal document where Arobs Group guarantees FWF UK's obligations to the buyer. Most UK public sector frameworks accept this as a remedy for EFS failure of a UK subsidiary

- Agree the standard PCG template with Arobs. CCS/GCA publishes a template PCG as Schedule 5 of the standard call-off contract, which can be used as a starting point

- Have Arobs's audited consolidated accounts available in English. Romanian accounts may need to be translated with a certified translation cover

- Pre-clear the arrangement with Arobs legal, so when a future bid requires PCG, the response is days not weeks

- Document the PCG approach in a reusable bid template, so future bid handlers do not have to reinvent it

Without this, every future UK public sector framework application risks the same EFS disregard. This is the lesson the G-Cloud 15 outcome forces FWF to learn.

## Alternative routes during the G-Cloud gap

While G-Cloud is the dominant cloud framework, it is not the only public sector route. During the gap from November 2026 to March 2028, alternatives to assess include:

- **RM6263 Digital Specialists and Programmes: **successor to Digital Outcomes and Specialists, suitable for digital and agile delivery work

- **RM6190 Technology Services 4: **broader technology services framework

- **NHS SBS frameworks: **NHS Shared Business Services runs its own framework agreements relevant to NHS buyers

- **HealthTrust Europe: **NHS-focused buying consortium with multiple framework agreements

- **GCA eMarketplace: **direct catalogue purchases below framework threshold

- **Find a Tender: **individual procurements outside of frameworks

- **Subcontracting to primes: **existing G-Cloud or DPS suppliers may need cloud support delivery capability

Each of these has its own EFS, application mechanics, and call-off rules. They need to be assessed individually against FWF UK's structure and offering.
