## The legal landscape

The Procurement Act 2023 came into force on 24 February 2025, replacing the Public Contracts Regulations 2015 (PCR 2015) for most new procurements. The Act introduces consolidated procedures, a new Central Digital Platform, and revised supplier exclusion regimes.

Two regimes now run in parallel:

- New procurements above threshold launched on or after 24 February 2025 follow the Procurement Act 2023

- Procurements launched before 24 February 2025 continue under PCR 2015 until they conclude. This includes G-Cloud 14, which is a PCR15 framework

G-Cloud 15 is the first G-Cloud iteration run under the Procurement Act 2023. Suppliers bidding into UK public sector now need to know both regimes during the transition period.

## Routes to market

UK public sector buyers can procure goods and services through several routes:

### Frameworks

Pre-competed agreements where suppliers compete once to get listed, then buyers can call off without further competition. Examples: G-Cloud, RM6190 Technology Services 4. Suitable for established, repeatable services.

### Dynamic Markets

Replacement for Dynamic Purchasing Systems (DPS) under the Procurement Act 2023. Open at any time for new suppliers to join. Suitable for diverse and evolving requirements.

### Open competitive procedures

Individual tenders for specific contracts. Published on Find a Tender for above-threshold procurements. Suitable for one-off large procurements.

### Competitive flexible procedure

New under the Procurement Act 2023, allows buyers to design bespoke procedures within the legal framework. Replaces several PCR 2015 procedures.

### Direct awards

Permitted under specific grounds (single supplier, urgency, etc.). Limited applicability in practice.

## G-Cloud specifically

G-Cloud is run by Government Commercial Agency (GCA, formerly Crown Commercial Service until 1 April 2026). It is a series of framework agreements for cloud-based services.

### G-Cloud 15 lot structure

- Lot 1a: Cloud Hosting (IaaS/PaaS)

- Lot 1b: Above Official (higher security classifications)

- Lot 2a: Specialist SaaS (iSaaS)

- Lot 2b: SaaS

- Lot 3: Cloud Support (consultancy, professional services)

G-Cloud 14 has fewer lots: Lot 1 Cloud Hosting, Lot 2 Cloud Software, Lot 3 Cloud Support. FWF's Microsoft Practice work fits Lot 3 in both iterations.

### How it works

- Supplier applies during a competition window

- GCA assesses against Conditions of Participation, including economic and financial standing

- Successful suppliers are listed on the Digital Marketplace catalogue with service definitions and pricing

- Buyers search the catalogue, shortlist (up to three suppliers required), and award call-off contracts

- Call-off contracts run independently of the framework, up to 48 months total

- Framework eventually expires and is replaced by the next iteration

### Open framework mechanics under G-Cloud 15

- Total framework period: 4 years

- Three sub-frameworks: two 18-month and one 12-month

- New suppliers can apply at defined re-entry windows

- Existing suppliers carry forward between sub-frameworks

## The bid lifecycle

End to end, the bid lifecycle is:

- **Opportunity identification: **monitor Find a Tender, Contracts Finder, and direct buyer engagement for relevant opportunities

- **Qualification: **assess whether the opportunity fits FWF's capability, scale, and commercial appetite

- **Bid/no-bid decision: **formal go/no-go based on qualification, capacity, and likelihood of winning

- **Response development: **draft the technical, financial, and supporting documentation

- **Internal review: **quality check, evidence verification, pricing sign-off

- **Submission: **submit through the buyer's portal before the deadline

- **Clarifications: **respond to buyer questions during the evaluation period

- **Evaluation: **buyer scores submissions against published criteria

- **Award notice and standstill: **buyer announces preferred supplier, mandatory standstill period before contract signature

- **Contract award: **final contract signed and work begins

The clarification step is critical and is precisely where the G-Cloud 15 submission failed. Active monitoring of bid portals and submission email addresses during the evaluation period is essential. Clarification deadlines are typically short (5-10 working days) and missing one is fatal to the submission.

## Evaluation under the Procurement Act 2023

Under PCR 2015, the standard evaluation basis was MEAT (Most Economically Advantageous Tender), weighing price and quality.

Under the Procurement Act 2023, the standard basis is MAT (Most Advantageous Tender), which broadens the assessment beyond economics to allow more flexibility in how non-financial benefits (social value, environmental impact, innovation) are weighted.

Practical implications:

- Buyers can place more weight on quality, social value, and innovation than under PCR 2015

- Suppliers need stronger evidence of social value delivery, not just commitment

- Carbon Reduction Plans are mandatory for procurements over certain thresholds

## Economic and Financial Standing (EFS)

EFS is the assessment of whether a supplier is financially capable of delivering the contract. It is part of the Conditions of Participation under the Procurement Act 2023 (Stage 3 of the G-Cloud 15 evaluation).

For G-Cloud 15, EFS thresholds are calibrated by lot and expected contract value, replacing the previous blanket "Bronze Plus" rating.

The standard EFS assessment looks at:

- Turnover (typically two times expected annual contract value as a minimum)

- Net assets and balance sheet strength

- Profitability

- Liquidity ratios

- Going concern status

For small UK subsidiaries of overseas groups, standalone accounts often fail EFS. The standard remedy is a Parent Company Guarantee.

## Parent Company Guarantee (PCG)

A PCG is a legal commitment by a parent company to perform the contract obligations of its subsidiary if the subsidiary defaults. For EFS purposes, it allows the buyer to assess the parent's financial standing instead of the subsidiary's.

For FWF UK Ltd, an Arobs Group PCG would substantially strengthen any UK public sector submission. Arobs is a publicly listed Romanian software group with audited consolidated accounts that would meet most EFS thresholds.

Practical steps:

- Engage Arobs legal and finance early to agree the principle

- Use the standard GCA PCG template as a starting point (typically Schedule 5 of the call-off contract)

- Have Arobs's audited consolidated accounts available, translated to English with certified translation if necessary

- Pre-clear the arrangement so it can be issued within days when needed for a bid

## Key terms glossary

| **CCS / GCA** | Crown Commercial Service / Government Commercial Agency (renamed 1 April 2026). The central UK procurement body |
| --- | --- |
| **Call-off contract** | An individual contract placed under a framework agreement |
| **Conditions of Participation** | The qualifying criteria a supplier must meet to bid (legal status, financial standing, exclusion grounds) |
| **DPS** | Dynamic Purchasing System (PCR 2015 term). Replaced by Dynamic Markets under PA23 |
| **EFS** | Economic and Financial Standing assessment |
| **FVRA** | Financial Viability Risk Assessment (the EFS check used in G-Cloud 15) |
| **Find a Tender** | UK service publishing above-threshold procurement notices |
| **Framework Agreement** | A pre-competed agreement allowing buyers to call off services without full retender |
| **MAT** | Most Advantageous Tender (PA23 evaluation basis) |
| **MEAT** | Most Economically Advantageous Tender (PCR 2015 evaluation basis) |
| **PA23** | Procurement Act 2023 |
| **PCG** | Parent Company Guarantee |
| **PCR 2015** | Public Contracts Regulations 2015 (predecessor regime) |
| **Standstill** | Mandatory waiting period between award notification and contract signature |
