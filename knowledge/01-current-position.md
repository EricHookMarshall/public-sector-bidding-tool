## Purpose

This document captures the state of FWF UK Ltd's public sector bidding position following the departure of the bid manager and the disregard of the G-Cloud 15 submission. It is intended as foundational context for ongoing work to stabilise and rebuild the bidding capability.

## Company structure

FWF (Future WorkForce UK Ltd) is the UK trading entity, a subsidiary of Arobs Group, a publicly listed Romanian software company. The UK entity is small: an MD, a sales director, and Romanian delivery staff recharged from the parent. The Microsoft Practice was established in September 2024 and covers Power Platform, Copilot Studio, Microsoft 365, and Azure AI consultancy.

This structure has direct implications for public sector bidding. FWF UK Ltd's standalone accounts will not pass Economic and Financial Standing (EFS) thresholds on most GCA frameworks, which is the foundational issue underlying the G-Cloud 15 disregard. The standard remedy is a Parent Company Guarantee from Arobs Group.

### What this means in practice

Two failures appear in the disregard letter: missing financial statements, and a missed clarification response. The most likely explanation is that the bid manager submitted FWF UK Ltd's standalone accounts, GCA issued a clarification request seeking additional financial evidence or a parent guarantee, and the request was not answered. The reason for the unanswered clarification could range from the bid manager leaving mid-process to the request landing in an unmonitored mailbox.

GCA never reached substantive evaluation of FWF's services, pricing, social value, or technical capability. The disregard is an administrative failure at the gate, not a quality judgement on the offering.

The 30-day formal challenge window under the Procurement Act 2023 closed in mid-April 2026. A discretionary reconsideration request is technically possible but unlikely to succeed. The practical focus needs to be on G-Cloud 14 utilisation and G-Cloud 15 re-entry rather than appeal.

## G-Cloud 15 mechanics and next opportunity

G-Cloud 15 is the first G-Cloud iteration run under the Procurement Act 2023. It operates as an open framework over a four-year period, with three sub-frameworks awarded across two 18-month terms and one 12-month term. New suppliers can apply at defined re-entry windows.

With G-Cloud 15 likely going live in September 2026, the practical re-entry windows for FWF are approximately:

- March 2028: 18 months after go-live, first re-entry window

- September 2029: 36 months after go-live, second re-entry window

Between G-Cloud 14 expiry (28 October 2026) and the first G-Cloud 15 re-entry window (around March 2028), FWF will have no live G-Cloud listing through which buyers can discover the practice. This is a 16-17 month gap that needs other commercial routes to bridge.
