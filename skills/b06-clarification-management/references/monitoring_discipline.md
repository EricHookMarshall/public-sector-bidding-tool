# Clarification monitoring — the discipline

The skill logs, flags and drafts. It does not watch the inbox. These rules do
the real work — they are what was missing when G-Cloud 15 failed.

## Non-negotiables

1. **Named owner + named backup** for the whole evaluation period. Ownership
   never sits with anyone who could leave mid-window. (Both key departures in
   the G-Cloud 15 window left clarifications unowned — the root cause.)

2. **Monitored shared mailbox**, not an individual's inbox, as the portal/
   submission contact. Individual inboxes go dark when people leave.
   - Live action: the G-Cloud 14 Digital Marketplace listing still shows a
     former employee as contact — update it to a monitored address now.

3. **Check the portal itself**, not just email — portals do not always notify.

4. **Assume short windows** (5–10 working days). Treat every request as urgent
   from the moment it lands. Set an internal due date earlier than the buyer's.

## Cadence

| When | Action |
|------|--------|
| Before evaluation | Confirm owner, backup, monitored mailbox. Create register. |
| Daily | Portal + mailbox check. `clarification_log.py list`. |
| Twice daily | Final week, or whenever any item is open. |
| On request | Log immediately. Draft. Sign off. Send before internal due. |
| Past escalation date | MD alerted — item cannot sit unowned. |
| At award/close | Confirm no open items; hand to B07. |

## The one-line lesson

G-Cloud 15 was not a commercial weakness — the fix was minor and the materials
existed. It was an ownership-and-monitoring failure. Do not let a clever skill
stack disguise that this step needs a person who is watching.
