# Answer patterns & framing

## Claim / evidence / benefit
1. **Claim** — what FWF does (specific, active).
2. **Evidence** — how it is proven (metric, prior delivery, certificate, named
   method). `[EVIDENCE: ...]` where a real fact must be inserted.
3. **Benefit** — what the buyer gets (outcome, risk reduced, value).

## Method / deliverable / assurance
For "describe your approach" questions: the actual method → the tangible
deliverable → how quality/timeline/risk is controlled.

## Win-theme framing
- Lead with the buyer outcome, not FWF history.
- Thread 2–3 consistent win themes through every answer.
- Mirror the evaluation criterion's language so scoring is effortless.

## FWF honesty guardrails
- Small UK team; Romanian delivery recharged from Arobs — frame capacity
  honestly through the Arobs relationship.
- Stay inside the Microsoft Practice envelope; anything outside needs a named
  partner basis.
- No invented metrics, clients, or certifications — placeholder, don't fabricate.
- Regime-correct: PA23/MAT; correct RM codes; GCA not CCS.

## Avoid
- Generic filler ("robust", "world-class", "synergies") with no substance.
- Answering an easier question than the one asked.
- Overrunning the limit.
- Claims with no evidence and no benefit.
- Reused text without the freshness checks (B03).
